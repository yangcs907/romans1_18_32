 Questions

The Unrighteousness of the Gentiles (1:18-32)

One
Romans 1:20 is reminiscent of Psalm 19:1 - "The heavens declare the glory of God". How do "the things that have been made" give clear evidence of God's "eternal power and divine nature"?

Two
Throughout the passage, Paul uses phrases like "God gave them up in the lusts of their hearts" (Romans 1:24), "God gave them up to dishonorable passions" (Romans 1:26), and "God gave them up to a debased mind" (Romans 1:28). What implications do such proclomations have for the idea of "free will"?

Three
If sinners "Suppress the truth (Romans 1:18)", how can Paul say that the truth is "clearly perceived" (Romans 1:20)?

Four
In this passage, Paul is revealing that even the irreligious - in this case, classified as Gentiles, or "non-Jews" - are aware of a holy standard. What are some ways in which even those who deny God demonstrate a tacit awareness of moral absolutes?
